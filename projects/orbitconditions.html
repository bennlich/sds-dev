Orbit Conditions

2014
Found 3D Models, Computer-generated Prints
Languages: Rhinoscript

Orbit Conditions was developed as part of Chris’s design MFA thesis. The project experimented with applying procedural design strategies to predetermined topics. In this case, the moon. The goal was to write a process to generate a form language which related to the content but could produce an infinite number of unique expressions.

The set of lunar spacecraft (also used for Moon in Google Earth) was taken from Google’s SketchUp 3D Warehouse. Chris imported the models into Rhino and wrote a RhinoScript to pick objects, generate the required random values, spin, project, and edit the resulting vectors. Because of the chance operations involved, and the ability to choose any 3D model, following these instructions should provide an infinite number of possible outcomes.
